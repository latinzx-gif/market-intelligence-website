# Summary: DataClaw Intelligence Report #004

**Topic:** High-Protein "Chewy" Student Snacks - Thailand 2026

## 1. The Core Insight
The 2026 Thai snack market is being redefined by **"Hefty Indulgence."** Students are moving away from traditional light snacks toward dense, high-protein "chewy" formats (e.g., Dubai-style cookies) that offer both high-sensory tactile experiences (ASMR) and functional nutritional value (5g-15g protein).

## 2. Sensory Efficacy
Texture is the new nutritional label. The combination of a mochi-like chewy base and a crunchy (Kataifi/Granola) filling serves as the primary proof of quality for Gen Z consumers. Brands that fail to deliver this sensory contrast are filtered out of the "viral" unboxing and unboxing cycle.

## 3. The Impulse Pivot
7-Eleven Thailand has successfully established the **50 THB impulse threshold** for functional snacks. By positioning premium chewy cookies (Grainey) at eye-level adjacent to coffee and focus drinks, they have normalized a ~60% price premium over standard protein bars for the student demographic.

## 4. Giant Modernization
Legacy brands are not being left behind. Glico and Lotte are utilizing protein-fortified "crushed" textures and collaborations with granola brands (e.g., Grainey x Koala's March) to stay relevant. The use of **AR packaging** to deliver student study tips represents a successful fusion of functional food and digital lifestyle.

## 5. Strategic Whitespace
The gap between ultra-premium artisanal cookies (~300 THB) and mass-market fortified biscuits (~25 THB) identifies an opportunity for **"Student-Premium" multi-packs** (sachets of 3-4 mini functional cookies) that offer the Dubai-style aesthetic at a 100-150 THB price point suitable for campus vending and bookstore retail.
