## Executive Summary
The 2026 student functional beverage market is entering a "Phase 2" shift. After years of focus on daytime alertness and cognitive "spiking" (Caffeine/Matcha), a significant whitespace has emerged: **Night-Time Biological Recovery.** 

Students are increasingly reporting "The Matcha Dilemma"—the inability to shut down the brain after 19:00 study sessions due to caffeine's long half-life. This has triggered a pivot toward **Savory Botanicals** (Miso, Koji, Hojicha) and **Circadian Nutrition**—drinks designed not to "induce sleep," but to "reset the system" for a high-performance tomorrow.

---

## 1. The Core Insight: The Savory Pivot
Our analysis of 2026 consumer sentiment reveals a rejection of sweet profiles for evening study support. Sweetened dairy and teas are being replaced by umami-rich, savory infusions.

- **The Hojicha Shift:** Hojicha (roasted green tea) has become the "Evening Matcha." Its low caffeine content combined with high aromatics is being used as a signal for the body to transition from study to rest.
- **Study Broths:** Artisanal brands (KOJIPOP, local Miso labs) are marketing "Functional Broths" containing Koji and GABA. These are positioned as healthy alternatives to instant noodles for late-night satiety without the glycemic spike.

## 2. Competitive Landscape: FMCG vs. Artisanal
The market is splitting into two distinct pricing and positioning tiers:

### Tier 1: FMCG Volume (Sappe, Tofusan)
- **Positioning:** Blue Light Recovery & Muscle Repair.
- **Key Products:** Tofusan High Protein Soy Milk (Lutein/Zeaxanthin) and Sappe Beauti Jelly (Chamomile/Magnesium).
- **Price Point:** 20-45 THB.
- **Target:** Mass student population seeking "Healthy Supper" replacements.

### Tier 2: Premium Functional (Artisanal Ferments)
- **Positioning:** Cognitive Reset & Cellular Recovery.
- **Key Products:** Fermented "Night Broths" and Sparkling Koji.
- **Price Point:** 85-120 THB.
- **Target:** Biohackers and high-performance students willing to pay for "Scientific" recovery.

## 3. Marketing Framework: Circadian Nutrition
Winning brands in 2026 are moving away from reactive "Sleep Aid" claims toward proactive "Sunset Rituals."

- **Science-First Hooks:** Terms like "Cellular Repair," "Autophagy Support," and "Cortisol Reset" are appearing on premium labels.
- **Visual Cues:** Packaging has shifted to deep indigo, matte textures, and "Sunset gradients" to communicate physiological transition.
- **Sugar-Free Baseline:** Following the Feb 2026 "Normal Sweetness" standards, 100% of emerging nighttime functional drinks are sugar-free or "Stevia-optimized."

## 4. Strategic Recommendations
1. **R&D:** Prioritize savory umami bases (Miso/Yeast extracts) over traditional floral/honey profiles for nighttime study lines.
2. **Ingredient Stack:** Combine blue-light recovery (Lutein) with neurological calm (GABA/Magnesium) to create a "Study-Recovery" basket.
3. **Naming:** Avoid "Sleep Drink" (implies medicine). Use "Recovery Infusion," "Sunset Reset," or "AM-Prep."
