# Summary: DataClaw Intelligence Report #001

**Topic:** Thailand Student Focus & Brain Drink Landscape 2026

## 1. The Core Insight
The Thai market for student brain health is bifurcated between **daily baseline nutrition** (multi-pack UHT milk with DHA/B12) and **acute performance support** (single-serve shots). Parents view these products as essential components of high-pressure exam-period "rituals," with convenience retail positioning shots at eye-level to meet this periodic demand.

## 2. Regulatory Reality
Compliance is absolute. Brands adhere strictly to the Thai FDA "Positive List" (Notification No. 447 B.E. 2566). Use of superlative language ("boosts intelligence", "best for brain") is restricted, leading to a highly standardized verbal landscape where brands compete on ingredient density and format rather than aggressive claim variance.

## 3. Market Landscape
- **Baseline UHT:** Dominanted by Hi-Q and Foremost. Price Per Serving (PPS) is optimized at ~19 THB via multi-pack digital sales.
- **Acute Shots:** Dominated by Peptein and Brand's Gen U. PPS is premium at ~31 THB, driven by convenience and single-serve glass bottle packaging.
- **Emerging Supplements:** Players like Mamarine and LAC are introducing "waterless" powder and gummy formats to capture the school-age segment beyond traditional dairy.

## 4. Retail Performance
In physical convenience channels (7-Eleven), cognitive products are grouped in high-visibility zones (8+ horizontal facings) at the 1.5m eye-level mark, emphasizing their status as impulse-driven functional aids rather than commodity grocery items.

## 5. Strategic Whitespace
The gap between "daily UHT" and "acute shots" suggests an opportunity for **hybrid formats** (e.g., student-specific sachets or multi-vitamin juices) that offer the performance claims of shots at a multi-pack price point suitable for the full exam-prep cycle.
