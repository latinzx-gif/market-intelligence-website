## Executive Summary
- **Consumer Rituals:** Thai parents provide functional beverages as specific supports during exam preparation seasons (M.1 admission).
- **Regulatory Guardrails:** Strict Thai FDA adherence is observed; brands utilize exact Positive List wording for Vitamin B12 claims.
- **Dual-Track Market:** The landscape is divided between multi-pack UHT dairy for baseline nutrition and single-serve shots for acute mental fatigue.
- **Retail Grouping:** Convenience channels (7-Eleven) group functional shots in high-visibility middle-upper refrigerated shelves.
- **Pricing Divide:** Student-positioned UHT dairy averages ~19 THB per serving, while premium functional shots average ~31 THB per serving.

## Section 1: Consumer Signals
**Objective:** Document the specific consumer language and situational context surrounding the use of functional beverages by students in Thailand.

**Observations:**
- Parents on Pantip cite the use of specific functional beverages during school entrance exam periods (e.g., M.1 admission).
- Consumer language describes functional drinks as providing nourishment when high mental energy is required.
- Consumer language associates product usage with providing encouragement for studying.
- Brands specifically mentioned in consumer discussions include Peptein and Brand's Gen U.

**Evidence:**
- **Source:** Pantip.com (Family/Education forums)
- **Quote:** "ช่วยให้เด็กรู้สึกว่าได้รับการบำรุงเป็นพิเศษในช่วงที่ต้องใช้พลังสมองเยอะ ทำให้มีกำลังใจในการอ่านหนังสือ"
- **Observed Product Mentions:** Peptein, Brand's Gen U.
- **Observed Context:** Preparation for competitive entrance exams and intense study sessions.

## Section 2: Regulatory Environment
**Objective:** Identify the mandatory legal standards and approved phrasing for health claims and food advertising in Thailand.

**Observations:**
- The Thai FDA maintains a pre-approved "Positive List" of nutrient function claims.
- Nutrient function claims for Vitamin B12 require a minimum threshold of 15% of the Thai RDI per serving.
- Specific warning statements are required for food advertisements targeting children.
- Advertising regulations are enforced through specific "Sor.Aor." (ส.อ.) criteria.

**Evidence:**
- **Regulation 1:** Notification No. 447 B.E. 2566. Wording: "วิตามินบี 12 มีส่วนช่วยในการทำงานตามปกติของระบบประสาทและสมอง"
- **Regulation 2:** Announcement B.E. 2564. Wording: "บริโภคแต่น้อยและออกกำลังกายเพื่อสุขภาพ"

## Section 3: Competitor Landscape
**Objective:** Map the ingredients, product formats, and marketing claims of student-focused dairy products.

**Observations:**
- Multiple brands (Hi-Q, Foremost) include DHA, Omega 3-6-9, and Vitamin B12 as functional ingredients.
- UHT carton is the dominant format for the dairy segment.
- Marketing claims focus on academic readiness and brain function using pre-approved wording.
- Supplement competitors (Mamarine, LAC) utilize "powder-to-mouth" and "gummy" formats.

**Evidence:**
- **Hi-Q S-KOOL:** DHA, Vitamin B12, Whey. Claim: "พร้อมเรียนรู้อย่างเต็มที่"
- **Foremost Omega:** DHA, Sphingomyelin, Choline. Claim: "ช่วยเสริมสร้างการทำงานของระบบประสาทและสมอง"
- **Mamarine DHA 2 GO:** Powder format. Claim: "Up-skill" learning and reduce brain fatigue.

## Section 4: Retail Execution
**Objective:** Document the physical and digital retail presence, pricing, and shelf placement.

**Observations:**
- UHT milk products are sold in bulk multi-packs online (12-pack, 36-pack).
- Functional shots are sold in single-serve 100ml bottles.
- Grouped eye-level shelf presence (8 adjacent facings) in 7-Eleven refrigerated cases.

**Evidence:**
- **Digital PPS:** UHT (~19 THB) vs. Shots (~31 THB).
- **Shelf Zone:** Middle-upper refrigerated shelves for cognitive products.

## Section 5: Observed Patterns
**Objective:** Synthesize repeated observations into foundational market patterns.

**Observations:**
- Dual-track strategy: Multi-pack UHT for baseline vs. single-serve shots for acute use.
- standardization of claims: Uniform adherence to FDA Positive List wording for Vitamin B12.
- Occasion-based consumption: Direct alignment between exam-period anxiety and retail shot visibility.
