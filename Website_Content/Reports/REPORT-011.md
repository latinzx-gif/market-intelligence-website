# DataClaw Intelligence Report #011

## Executive Summary
The "Cognitive Hub" within 7-Eleven Thailand has evolved from a niche supplement corner into a sophisticated, multi-tiered **Performance Architecture**. By utilizing strategic vertical and horizontal blocking, retailers are successfully bridging the gap between high-trust traditional rituals and modern functional beverages.

This report maps the shelf hierarchy that defines the cognitive category, identifying the **"Middle-Shelf Bridge"** as the primary zone of growth for the 2027 market cycle.

## 1. The Shelf Hierarchy: Ritual to Refreshment
7-Eleven categorizes performance products into a three-tier vertical structure that dictates consumer perception.

| Tier | Shelf Position | Category | Core Benefit | Price Range |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Upper** | Eye-Level+ | Traditional Supplements | High-Trust / Ritual | 42 - 70 THB |
| **Middle** | Hand-Level | Functional Bridges | Daily Focus / Habit | 15 - 25 THB |
| **Lower** | Knee-Level | Mass Refreshment | General Energy | 10 - 15 THB |

## 2. Strategic Adjacency: The "Middle-Shelf Bridge"
The grouping of **B-Vitamin fortified beverages** directly adjacent to concentrated shots is the most significant shift observed.

- **Normalization:** Placing 20 THB drinks next to 45 THB shots makes the category feel "accessible" for daily use.
- **Vertical dominance:** **Brand's** utilizes vertical columns to anchor the section, while new entrants use horizontal row-blocking to establish sub-categories (e.g., Eye Health).

## 3. Strategic Verdict / Recommendations
1. **Placement Strategy:** Negotiate for **"Middle-Shelf Bridge"** placement. Positioning as a "Daily Enabler" drives higher volume than the "Emergency Exam Aid" upper shelf.
2. **Visual Scannability:** Packaging must be optimized for **Vertical Reading**. Labels should use high-contrast "Power Words" (FOCUS, ALPHA) legible from 3 feet.
3. **Ladder Strategy:** Launch a low-barrier B-Vitamin drink to drive trial and a high-margin concentrated shot to capture high-intent users.
