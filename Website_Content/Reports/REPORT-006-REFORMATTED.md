- Average Thai student under 22 uses screens 8h 24m daily.
- Digital Eye Strain prevalence is 80.4%.
- Market is shifting from generic berry positioning to retinal defense.

### 📌 Evidence
> "Thailand is the 4th most digitally active nation globally."
>
> "the average Thai student (under 22) spends **8 hours and 24 minutes** per day on digital screens."
>
> "This extreme exposure has triggered a **80.4% prevalence of Digital Eye Strain (DES)**."
>
> "We are witnessing a structural shift toward **\"Retinal Armor\"**"

### 💡 Strategic Implication
Category demand is moving toward high-efficacy eye-defense products with explicit physiological relevance.

### Executive Takeaway
Screen-time overload has created a large and urgent recovery-led demand pocket.

## 4. Regulatory Heatmap

Claim rules create a high barrier and force competition toward compliant science positioning.

### 🎯 Objective
Clarify the claim boundary that defines commercial viability.

### 🔍 Key Findings
- Thai FDA Notification No. 477 tightens medical claim pathways.
- Pre-approved claims center on maintenance and support.
- Blue light filtration requires clinical dossier.
- Bilberry plus 10mg lutein aligns with high-weight fatigue segments.

### 📌 Evidence
> "The Thai FDA (Notification No. 477) has created a high barrier to entry for \"Medical\" claims"
>
> "Pre-Approved: \"Maintenance of Vision,\" \"Cornea Health,\" \"Night Vision Support.\""
>
> "Restricted: \"Blue Light Filtration\" (requires clinical dossier), \"IQ Enhancement,\" \"Autism Correction.\""

### 💡 Strategic Implication
Compliant claim architecture is now as critical as ingredient architecture.

### Executive Takeaway
Regulatory precision is a direct source of competitive advantage.
