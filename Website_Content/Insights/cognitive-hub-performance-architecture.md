# The "Cognitive Hub" - Mapping the 7-Eleven Performance Architecture

## What Happened
Retail audits of 7-Eleven Thailand reveal a highly structured "Cognitive Hub" strategy. Traditional supplements (Brand's/Scotch Essence of Chicken) occupy the upper shelves as high-trust, high-price "ritual" products. These are horizontally flanked by concentrated cognitive shots (Peptein, Brand's Gen U). Crucially, the middle shelf acts as a "Cognitive Bridge," featuring B-Vitamin fortified beverages (C-vitt Focus, B-ing) that offer a more approachable entry point for younger consumers.

## Why It Matters
The 7-Eleven shelf layout is the primary engine of category discovery in Thailand. By placing B-Vitamin drinks adjacent to traditional cognitive shots, 7-Eleven is normalizing "Cognitive Performance" as a daily, beverage-led habit rather than just a high-stakes exam ritual. This layout reduces the barrier to entry for the "Cognitive" category, moving it from the pharmacy-style upper shelf to the daily-refreshment middle shelf.

## Strategic Implications
- **Category Blurring:** The line between "Supplements" and "Functional Beverages" is disappearing at the point of sale.
- **Brand-Blocking Power:** Vertical blocks for legacy brands (Brand's) remain dominant, but category-based horizontal blocks (B-Vitamins) are where the growth in variety is occurring.
- **Price Laddering:** The shelf layout provides a clear "Price Ladder"—from the 15-20 THB B-Vitamin drinks to the 35-50 THB concentrated shots.

## Recommendations
- **Shelf-Placement Strategy:** New entrants should target the "Middle-Shelf Bridge" (adjacent to B-Vitamins) to capture daily-habit consumers rather than trying to compete on the crowded "High-Stakes" upper shelf.
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Packaging must be designed for "Vertical Scanability" to fit into the brand-blocked columns of major retailers.
- **Cross-Category Sampling:** Brands with both shots and beverages should use "Adjacent Blocking" to encourage trade-up from low-price drinks to high-margin shots.
